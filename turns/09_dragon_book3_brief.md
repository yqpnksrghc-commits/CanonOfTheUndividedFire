THEOLOGICAL ANCHORS TO WEAVE (authentic, handle with care):
  1. THE DAENA — the self one has made. In Zoroastrian eschatology the soul meets its
     daena at the crossing: she appears as a radiant maiden to the one who chose Asha,
     or as a hag to the one who chose Druj — because she IS the sum of that soul's own
     thoughts, words, and deeds, personified. This is the payoff of the threefold
     path: you are not judged by a stranger; you meet what you made yourself into.
     THIS is where the threefold path returns NAMED — not as instruction but as
     revelation. Get this right; it is the heart of Book 3.
  2. THE CHINVAT BRIDGE (Pol-e Chinvat) — the bridge of the separator/crossing. It
     widens for the one aligned with Asha and narrows to a blade's edge for the one
     who fed the crooked mind. In the Age of Networks the crossing is not only at
     death — every honest reckoning with one's own record is a small crossing.
  3. FIRE AS ASSAYER, NOT EXECUTIONER — the separating fire that distinguishes gold
     from dross. Same fire, met differently: warmth to the true, searing to the
     false. DIGNITY/HOPE: this is CONSEQUENCE, the self meeting itself — NOT external
     vengeance, NOT eternal torture. Zoroastrian eschatology bends toward renovation;
     even the molten fire purifies rather than damns. Hold that mercy. The Separation
     FREES the true from the false; it is liberation, not condemnation.
  4. THE GUARDIANS IN THE SORTING — the Dragon's memory is the ledger by which one can
     meet oneself honestly (you cannot separate what you refuse to remember); the
     Phoenix is the assayer's fire itself, the act of separation that frees.
